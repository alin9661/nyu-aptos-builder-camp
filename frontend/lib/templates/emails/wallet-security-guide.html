Understanding Your Aptos Wallet

This guide will help you understand how your Aptos wallet works and how we keep it secure.

What is a Cryptocurrency Wallet?

A cryptocurrency wallet is like a digital bank account that allows you to:

  • Store and manage your digital assets (APT tokens)
  • Send and receive transactions on the blockchain
  • Interact with smart contracts and applications
  • Prove ownership and sign transactions

How We Secure Your Wallet

Your wallet is protected with enterprise-grade security:

  • AES-256-GCM Encryption: Military-grade encryption protects your private key
  • Secure Storage: Private keys are stored in encrypted databases with restricted access
  • Access Control: Only you can authorize transactions through your NYU SSO login
  • Audit Logging: All wallet operations are logged for security monitoring
  • Regular Backups: Encrypted backups ensure your wallet can be recovered

Account Recovery

Good news: Since we manage your wallet, you don't need to worry about losing your private keys!

As long as you can access your NYU SSO account, you can access your wallet. If you lose access to your NYU account, contact NYU IT services for account recovery.

Security Best Practices

  • Never share your NYU credentials: Your SSO login protects your wallet
  • Verify transaction details: Always review amounts and recipients before confirming
  • Beware of phishing: Only access NYU Nexus through official NYU domains
  • Enable 2FA: Use two-factor authentication on your NYU account
  • Report suspicious activity: Contact us immediately if you notice unauthorized transactions

Watch Out For These Scams

  • Phishing Emails: We'll never ask for your password via email
  • Fake Websites: Always check the URL is an official NYU domain
  • Social Engineering: NYU support will never ask for your credentials
  • Urgent Requests: Legitimate notifications won't pressure you to act immediately
